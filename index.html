Sam's School Trip

Sam's class visited the town museum last Tuesday. They arrived at ten o'clock in the morning. Their guide, Mrs Chen, met them at the entrance and gave everyone a map.

The first room they entered was the Ancient Egypt gallery. Near the door stood a tall stone statue of a pharaoh. In the centre of the room, there was a mummy in a glass case. The label said it was more than 3,500 years old!

Next, the children went upstairs to the Prehistoric Gallery. Sam's favourite exhibit was a complete dinosaur skeleton displayed in the middle of the room. The guide explained that this type of dinosaur ate plants and lived about 150 million years ago.

After their lunch break, the class visited the Medieval Hall. They saw armour, shields, and swords used by knights long ago. A large painting of a famous battle hung on the wall.

At the end of the visit, each student wrote three facts in their notebook. Sam couldn't wait to tell her family about everything she had seen!
